<p><p>The natural food color additives market is divided into various types to cater to different consumer preferences. Carotenoids are pigments derived from plants and are commonly used in yellow and orange food coloring. Turmeric oleoresin, made from turmeric roots, imparts a vibrant yellow color. Enocianina is a natural blue coloring extracted from the petals of the butterfly pea flower. Paprika oleoresin is derived from paprika peppers and provides a deep red color. Spirulina extract, obtained from a type of algae, is known for its blue-green hue. Chlorophyll, found in green plants, is used for green coloring. Carmine is a red pigment derived from insects. Other natural food color additives include fruit and vegetable extracts, annatto, and beet juice powder.</p></p>
