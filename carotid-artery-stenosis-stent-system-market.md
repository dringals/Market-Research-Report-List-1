<p><p>The Carotid Artery Stenosis Stent System is a medical device designed to treat carotid artery stenosis, which is the narrowing of the carotid arteries that supply blood to the brain. This condition can lead to an increased risk of stroke, and the stent system is intended to widen the blocked or narrowed artery in order to restore normal blood flow.</p><p>The stent system consists of a metal mesh tube that is placed inside the carotid artery to support the artery walls and keep them open. The stent is typically deployed using a minimally invasive procedure called angioplasty, where a catheter is threaded through a small incision in the groin or arm to reach the blocked artery.</p><p>The market for carotid artery stenosis stent systems is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.3% during the forecasted period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Additionally, increasing healthcare expenditure and a rising demand for minimally invasive procedures are also contributing to the market growth.</p><p>However, the market is not without its challenges.
