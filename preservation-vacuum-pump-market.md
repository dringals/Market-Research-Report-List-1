<p><p>Preservation vacuum pumps are used to remove air or gases from sealed containers or systems, maintaining a vacuum environment for preservation purposes. There are several types of preservation vacuum pumps available in the market. </p><p>Diaphragm type pumps utilize a flexible diaphragm and valves to create a vacuum. They are compact, oil-free, and suitable for small-scale applications. </p><p>Piston pumps use reciprocating pistons to create a vacuum by compressing and expanding gas. They are robust and suitable for larger-scale industrial applications. </p><p>Liquid ring type pumps use a liquid ring (typically water) to create a vacuum by rotating an impeller. They offer constant seal liquid supply and are suitable for handling wet gases. </p><p>Other types of preservation vacuum pumps include rotary vane pumps and scroll pumps, which operate based on different mechanisms. These pumps provide a wide range of options for various preservation vacuum needs.</p></p>
