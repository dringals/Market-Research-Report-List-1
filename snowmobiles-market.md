<p><p>Snowmobiles can be categorized into two market types based on their engine technology: two-stroke and four-stroke engines. Two-stroke engines are more compact and lightweight, requiring a fuel and oil mixture for lubrication. They provide higher power-to-weight ratio but are less fuel-efficient and emit more emissions. On the other hand, four-stroke engines are larger and heavier but offer better fuel efficiency and reduced emissions. They run on gasoline alone, eliminating the need for oil mixing. Both market types cater to different consumer preferences and offer unique features to enhance the snowmobiling experience.</p></p>
