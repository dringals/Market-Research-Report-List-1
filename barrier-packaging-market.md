<p><p>Barrier packaging refers to the use of specific materials to protect products from external factors such as moisture, gas, odor, and light. Different types of materials are used for barrier packaging, including PET (Polyethylene terephthalate), CPP (Cast Polypropylene), BOPP (Biaxially Oriented Polypropylene), PVA (Polyvinyl Alcohol), PLA (Polylactic Acid), and others. PET is known for its excellent gas barrier properties, while CPP offers good moisture and oxygen barrier. BOPP provides high clarity and stiffness. PVA is highly soluble in water, making it suitable for products requiring water solubility. PLA is a biodegradable polymer. Other materials may include aluminum foil, ethylene vinyl alcohol (EVOH), and nylon.</p></p>
